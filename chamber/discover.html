History

Brookshire was named in honor of Nathan Brookshire, who was a large land owner in the vicinity and a leading resident. He was a captain in the Texas army and participated in the storming and capture of Bexar in 1835. In 1893 John Kellner donated land and filed a plat for the Town of Kellner out of the William Cooper Survey, one of the oldest headrights in the state. Also in 1893, John Brookshire and O. C. Drew filed their plat for the Town of Brookshire out of the H. H. Pennington Survey. On May 24, 1946, The City of Brookshire was incorporated, comprised of both the Kellner and the Brookshire/Drew plats.

Location

The Brookshire area is situated ideally. Just 30 minutes from Downtown Houston, we are close enough to the throb of big city life to enjoy the best it has to offer but still very much a part of rural America. The area is rich in churches, civic organizations, and school and youth activities.

Desirability

The Brookshire area has emerged as one of Greater Houston's prime surburban areas. Brookshire boasts excellent school districts, afordable cost of living with abundant housing options as well as a variety of shopping and dining. We also have plenty of entertainment, ranging from beautiful parks to multiple festival to athletic events and activities.
